Interview Transcript – Beauty Salon Management System

Consultant: Hello, Isabella. I would like to understand your beauty salon business better so I can propose a suitable software solution. What services do you offer?

Isabella: We offer haircuts, coloring, hairstyling, manicure, pedicure, makeup, facial treatments, and eyebrow services.

Consultant: How are appointments currently managed?

Isabella: Mostly by phone calls, Instagram messages, and sometimes handwritten notes in a notebook.

Consultant: What problems do you face with this process?

Isabella: We sometimes double-book customers, forget appointments, or lose track of cancellations. It is also difficult to see which employee is available.

Consultant: Do different employees provide different services?

Isabella: Yes. Some employees do hair only, some do nails, and some do multiple services.

Consultant: How do you manage customer information?

Isabella: We do not have a structured system. We usually remember regular customers, but sometimes contact information or service history is missing.

Consultant: How do you handle payments?

Isabella: Payments are handled manually. We accept cash and card, but there is no central system for tracking daily revenue.

Consultant: Do you need reminders or notifications?

Isabella: Yes, appointment reminders would help reduce no-shows. Notifications for cancellations and schedule changes would also be useful.

Consultant: What reports would be useful for you as the salon owner?

Isabella: I would like to see daily and monthly revenue, most popular services, busiest employees, and appointment statistics.

Consultant: Would customers book online?

Isabella: That would be useful, but it is optional for the first version.

Consultant: Who would use the system internally?

Isabella: I would use it as the owner. Reception staff would manage bookings. Employees should see their schedules. Customers could optionally book online.

Consultant: What is the main goal of the system?

Isabella: To organize appointments better, reduce mistakes, manage employees and payments, and improve customer experience.
